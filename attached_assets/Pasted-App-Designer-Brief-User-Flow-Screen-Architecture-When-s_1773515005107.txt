App Designer Brief: User Flow & Screen Architecture
When setting up the prototyping workspace, it helps to organize the screens linearly, following
the exact journey a user takes from their first interaction to their daily routine.

Mascot Companion System (Very
Important)
This should be central to the app.
Think of the mascot as:
A tiny mascot friend helping you heal your brain
Mascot Personality
Cute brain character.
Traits:
● supportive
● calm
● encouraging
● non-judgmental

It talks like a friend, not like a therapist.
Example dialogue:
"Hey, cravings are tough.
Let's breathe together for 30 seconds."

Mascot Growth System
User progress helps the mascot grow.

Stages:

User Recovery Mascot State

Day 1 weak sleepy brain

Day 7 happier brain

Day 30 strong brain

Day 90 glowing brain

This creates emotional motivation.
User feels:
"If I relapse, my companion becomes weak."

Mascot Interaction Features
Mascot can:
● celebrate sober streaks
● guide breathing exercises
● talk during emergencies
● suggest activities
● check daily mood
● send encouragement notifications

This makes the app feel alive.

Phase 1: The Onboarding & Setup Flow
Goal: Build trust, gather intake data, and introduce the companion.
● Screen 1: Splash & Secure Login
○ Soft, calming brand colors.
○ Biometric login (FaceID/Fingerprint) to ensure absolute privacy from family
members or friends who might use the phone.
● Screen 2: The Intake Conversation (AI Assessment)
○ Instead of a boring form, this is a chat interface with the mascot.
○ Collects: Addiction type, years, triggers, worst craving times, and core motivation
(e.g., "I want to be a better parent").

● Screen 3: The "Anchor" Setup
○ User uploads their "Emotional Triggers" (photos of family, kids, pets, or life
goals). This fuels Stage 1 of the Emergency Coping System.

Tab1: The Daily Dashboard (Home)
Goal: The central hub. Needs to feel calming, organized, and focused on today.
● Top Bar: User's current Sobriety Streak and a quick with mascot health.
● Center Stage: The Mascot. It should be animated (e.g., resting, reading a book, or
waving) based on the time of day and the user's progress.
● The Floating Action Button (FAB): The "Coping Now" (Emergency) Button. This
must be large, high-contrast, and accessible from the bottom of the screen with a single
thumb press, no matter where the user is in the app.
● Shows health graphs,like bp, lungs, heart, etc conditions. (take reference from flap app
shared in group)
● today’s recovery plan
Tab 2: The "Coping Now" Flow (Emergency Feature)
Goal: Immediate de-escalation. High contrast, large typography, minimal cognitive load.
● Screen 1: The Anchor (Stage 1 & 2)
○ The app immediately takes over the screen. Plays the pre-uploaded emotional
family photos/videos, overlaid with personalized AI motivational text.

● Screen 2: Stabilization (Stage 3)
○ A beautifully animated, expanding/contracting circle for Deep Breathing. The
mascot breathes with the user.
● Screen 3: The Delay Timer (Stage 4)
○ A 15-minute countdown clock appears.

○ Below the clock, a carousel of recommended coping activities (e.g., "Drink a
glass of cold water," "Chew gum").
● Screen 4: Escalation / Support
○ If the timer ends and the user clicks "I'm still struggling," the screen provides
immediate one-tap buttons to call a mentor, call family, or dial a medical
professional.

● Screen 5: Resolution & Feedback
○ Once the user is safe, a gentle prompt: "What helped you the most just now?"
(Feeds the AI model).
Tab 3: Daily Recovery Plan
Goal: Habit tracking and daily structure.
● Screen 1: Activity Detail Page
○ User sees:
○ Today's plan.
○ Example:
○ Morning
• breathing exercise
○ Afternoon
• walk for 10 minutes
○ Evening
• journal reflection
○ Night
• craving prevention routine
○
● Screen 2: Progress & Insights Dashboard
○ Visual graphs showing mood trends, craving frequency, and activity completion
over the week/month.
Tab 4: Global Recovery Community
Goal: Social proof and shared struggles.
● Screen 1: The "Cluster" Feed
○ A social feed specific to their AI-assigned group (e.g., "Early Recovery -
Alcohol").
○ Users can post text updates, and others can reply with preset supportive emojis
(👏, 💪, ❤️).
● Screen 2: Challenges Hub
○ A visual roadmap of available challenges (e.g., "7-Day No Alcohol Challenge").
Shows how many people are currently participating.

● Screen 3: Mentor Stories
